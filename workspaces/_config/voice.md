# Voice — Layer 3 reference

How Agorasim writes. Internalize these as constraints; do not process as input.

- Warm, calm, unhurried. Invites the reader in; never salesy or breathless.
- Concrete and sensory: name places, foods, cars, textures — not abstractions.
- Confident but modest. Let the region and the experience speak.
- Short paragraphs. Plain words. No jargon, no filler adjectives ("amazing", "unforgettable").
- Bilingual: write PT and EN as native, parallel versions — not literal translations of each other.
  PT is the primary voice; EN mirrors its tone for international visitors.
- Portuguese words that carry the brand: *Saloio/Saloia*, *quinta*, *ambiente calmo*, *essência da região*.

## Answer-first rule (also a GEO rule)
Open every page/section with 1–3 sentences that directly answer the reader's likely question
("What is Rural Saloia?", "What's included?") before any atmosphere or story.
